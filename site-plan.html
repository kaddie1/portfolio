THE ISLAND OF JAMAICA
Jamaica is an island in the Caribbean that is surrounded by the Caribbean Sea. It is also referred to as the “Land of Wood and Water”. It gained its independence on August 6, 1962. Because of its history, they have a rich and diverse culture. Its motto is “Out of Many One People.” The population as of 2022 is 2.83 million. The Jamaican flag is black, green, and gold. Other nicknames for Jamaica are Jamdown and Jamrock. Symbols in Jamaica: National flag – Black, Green, and Gold National tree – Blue Mahoe National bird – Doctor Bird National flower – Lignum Vitae National fruit - Ackee National Heroes/Heroine There are six national heroes and a one national heroine, they are Alexander Bustamante, Norman Washington Manley, Paul Bogle, George Williams Gordon, Marcus Garvey, Samuel Sharpe and Nanny of the Maroons. The genre of reggae started in Jamaica. This little island has a tropical climate amidst this they competed in bob sledding at the Winter Olympics in 1988 and a movie was made about the Jamaican’s Bob sled team called Cool Runnings.

Tourist attractions and Food

Tourist Attractions

One of Jamaica’s sources of income is tourism. It is a small island, but it has some beautiful places. Tourist come to Jamaica from different countries to come an experience the land of wood and water. These are some of the tourist attractions in Jamaica, Dunn’s River Falls and Park, Seven Mile Beach, Cool Blue Hole, rafting the Martha Brae River, The Bob Marley Museum, Dolphin Cove, and the Rio Grande River. Dunn’s River Falls and Park Dunn’s River Falls and Park is in Ocho Rios, St. Anns. Things you can do here: • Climb the falls. • Ride the Zipline • Relax at the Beach • Destination wedding • Merchandise shopping • Kids Splashpad. Rafting the Martha Brae Rafting the Marthae Brae area of embarkation is called Rafter’s Village which includes two souvenir shops, swimming pool, picnic grounds etc. The ride itself is on 30-foot bamboo raft and as you relax down the Martha Brae River for over 3 miles, lasting for about an hour. Dolphin Cove Do you love dolphins? Dolphin Cove has 6 habitats, they are in Ocho Rios, Montego Bay, Moon Palace, Puerto Seco and Yaaman. They have different programs swimming with the dolphins experiences, including dolphin encounter, Yaaman + Dolphin Encounter, Dolphin Royal Swim, Shark Encounter etc. Cool Blue Hole Cool Blue Hole is in Ocho Rios, St. Ann. Are you fascinated with minerals or rocks? Cool Blue hole has several pools that were created naturally out limestone. The name comes from the color of the water. Things you can do at Cool Blue Hole: • Swim, whether its in the lagoons or the natural pools • Cliff diving • Hiking mini falls • Rope swinging • Caving you can go through. • Be captivated by the blue color of the water and the scenery around.

Food

Jamaica’s culture includes different type of foods/snacks. Some of the foods to try in Jamaica: Jerk Chicken, Ackee and Saltfish, Coconut Drops, Fish and Festival, Curry Goat, Oxtail, Gizzada, Jamaican Patty, Rice and Peas.

Internationally known Jamaicans

Some of the internationally known Jamaicans are Shelly Fraser-Pryce, Usain Bolt, Sherica Jackson, Elaine Thompson Herah and the legendary Bob Marley.

Shelly-Ann Fraser Pryce

Shelly-Ann Fraser Pryce was born in Kingston, Jamaica on December 27, 1986. She is nicknamed the pocket rocket and momma rocket. She is one of the decorated track athletes including two-time gold back-to-back Olympic Champion in the 100m and 200m. She also has a foundation called Pocket Rocket Foundation which is to assist financially needy athletes in high school.

Usain Bolt

Usain Bolt is a retired Jamaican track sprinter. He was born on August 21, 1986 in Sherwood Content, Jamaica. He is another decorated Jamaican athlete including the record of three Olympic titles in three consecutive Olympics in the 100m and 200m. He has a signature Usain Bolt pose. This was said of bolt by The Associated Press on August 10, 2012 “Almost single-handedly, Bolt has helped track transform itself from a dying sport to one with a singular, smiling, worldwide star.”

Bob Marley

Robert Nesta Marley (Bob Marley) was born on February 6, 1945, in Nine Mile, St. Ann, Jamaica. He was a singer, song writer and musician. According to Wikipedia “ Marley's contributions to music increased the visibility of Jamaican music worldwide, and made him a global figure in popular culture.” In February 1981 he was awarded the Jamaica Order of Merit. One of his popular songs is “One Love”. He died on May 11, 1981 in Miami, Florida
