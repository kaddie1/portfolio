Some of the internationally known Jamaicans are Shelly Fraser-Pryce, Usain Bolt, Sherica Jackson, Elaine Thompson Herah and the legendary Bob Marley.

Bob Marley

Robert Nesta Marley (Bob Marley) was born on February 6, 1945, in Nine Mile, St. Ann, Jamaica. He was a singer, song writer and musician. According to Wikipedia “ Marley's contributions to music increased the visibility of Jamaican music worldwide, and made him a global figure in popular culture.” In February 1981 he was awarded the Jamaica Order of Merit. One of his popular songs is “One Love”. He died on May 11, 1981 in Miami, Florida

Usain Bolt

Usain Bolt is a retired Jamaican track sprinter. He was born on August 21, 1986 in Sherwood Content, Jamaica. He is another decorated Jamaican athlete including the record of three Olympic titles in three consecutive Olympics in the 100m and 200m. He has a signature Usain Bolt pose. This was said of bolt by The Associated Press on August 10, 2012 “Almost single-handedly, Bolt has helped track transform itself from a dying sport to one with a singular, smiling, worldwide star.”

Shelly-Ann Fraser Pryce

Shelly-Ann Fraser Pryce was born in Kingston, Jamaica on December 27, 1986. She is nicknamed the pocket rocket and momma rocket. She is one of the decorated track athletes including two-time gold back-to-back Olympic Champion in the 100m and 200m. She also has a foundation called Pocket Rocket Foundation which is to assist financially needy athletes in high school.
